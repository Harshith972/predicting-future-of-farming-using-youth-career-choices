THE FUTURE OF FARMING

Analyzing the Career Aspirations of India's Youth

The Big Question: Is Farming A Future?

Based on a survey of 1,000 young individuals across India, we explored their interest in pursuing agriculture. The results reveal a complex picture of opportunity, hesitation, and competing ambitions.

Overall Interest

Respondents With Farming Background

62%

Family Owns Agricultural Land

55%

4.5 acres

The Demographic Divide

Interest in agriculture is not uniform. It shifts significantly based on geography, gender, and education, revealing how social and environmental contexts shape career outlooks.

Interest by Location

Rural youth show the highest interest, but a significant portion in urban areas are also considering agriculture, hinting at a potential shift towards modern AgriTech.

Interest by Education Level

Interest remains relatively consistent across education levels, declining only slightly at the postgraduate level where other specialized career opportunities may become more prominent.

The Influence of Background

Family history and economic realities, like land ownership and parents' occupation, are powerful predictors of a young person's inclination towards farming.

Impact of Land Ownership

Access to land is a critical factor. Those whose families own land are substantially more likely to consider a future in farming.

Influence of Parents' Occupation

Unsurprisingly, individuals with parents in Farming or Agricultural Labor show the highest interest. However, even children of parents in other sectors show a notable curiosity.

Barriers & New Horizons

For the 40% of youth not interested in farming, their reasons highlight key challenges the agricultural sector must address. Their alternative career interests also reveal the competitive landscape for talent.

Top Reasons for Avoiding Farming

Economic instability and a preference for urban lifestyles are the dominant deterrents, followed by the high perceived risks associated with climate and pests.

Most Desired Alternative Careers

The allure of the tech industry (Software/IT, Data Science) is strong, alongside stable government jobs. AgriTech itself appears as a promising bridge between traditional farming and modern aspirations.
